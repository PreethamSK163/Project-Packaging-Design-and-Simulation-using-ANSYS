> Wire Bond Creation, Material Assignment & Mold Compound Finalization in ANSYS AEDT

<h2>🔍 Overview</h2>

- Created wire bonds connecting die bond pads to substrate pads using JEDEC 4-point profile geometry — assigning gold material for accurate electrical and thermal simulation of the interconnect layer.
- Completed the full package cross-section model by applying the mold compound encapsulation layer — encapsulating the die, wire bonds, and bond pads to finalize the structure for reliability and thermal simulation.

<h2>⚙️ Tasks Covered</h2>

| Task | Description |
|:---|:---|
| Wire Bond Creation & Material Assignment | JEDEC profile wire geometry, gold material assignment, and alignment verification |
| Mold Compound & Package Finalization | Encapsulant geometry, material assignment, and final model verification |

<h2>📝 Stage Details</h2>

**Task 1 — Wire Bond Creation & Material Assignment** &nbsp;|&nbsp; `Wire Bond` `JEDEC Profile` `Gold` `Q3D`

Used the bond wire creation tool in AEDT to define wire bond geometry — selecting die bond pads and corresponding substrate pads, then specifying wire diameter, height, and the JEDEC 4-point profile to accurately model the physical shape and routing of each wire. Assigned gold material from the material library to all wire bonds — chosen for its excellent electrical conductivity and reliability in packaging applications. Adjusted wire bond properties and orientation to ensure correct alignment between die and substrate pads. Verified placement using 3D visualization tools to confirm mechanical integrity and electrical connectivity of the complete wire bond network.

**Task 2 — Mold Compound & Package Finalization** &nbsp;|&nbsp; `Mold Compound` `Epoxy` `Encapsulation` `Model Verification`

Created a rectangular solid to represent the mold compound — sized to fully encapsulate the die, wire bonds, and bond pads. Assigned epoxy mold compound material properties to accurately simulate its protective and insulating characteristics. Used the properties panel to adjust dimensions for complete coverage with no gaps. Reviewed the model tree to confirm all components were correctly organized and named. Performed final 3D inspection to verify the complete package cross-section — from substrate through die attach, bond pads, wire bonds, to the mold compound — preparing the model for subsequent thermal and mechanical reliability simulations.
